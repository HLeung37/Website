Crime Sequels by Hiu Yin Haley Leung

The goal of this project was to observe the data set of crimes from 2020 and 2021 to further predict if crime will rise or decrease. This would help lead to a better understanding of crimes that might not be broadcasted on everyday news. Letting everyone be more weary of their surroundings.

Comparison of Crime (Venn)

These two venn diagrams are created based on the amount of difference of crimes a male has commited comparitively to a female. Though these two diagrams don't give much insight, it shows that a majority of the crime a male commits is also commited by a female as well. With an overwhelming similarity of 175-176 crimes. This helps break the assumption that maybe one gender is less likely to commit a crime than the other. Make sure when walking outside to always look left and right, never assuming that you might be safe.

Comparison of Crime (Pie Chart)

The two pie charts on the left shows the percentage of crime each borough commited towards the total amount. All the boroughs seems to have not changed all that much with the percentages they contributed. The biggest difference would be Manhattan having more crime with an 1.8% increase. The lowest difference would be Queens having .1% less crime. Based on these two model it could be concluded that crimes happening in 2022, in terms of percentages from each borough will stay about the same.

Comparison of Crime (Histogram)

The two images on the right are the amount of crimes which each age groups performed.
 It is interesting to see the amount of crimes soar for the 25-44 age group. In 2020 the amount of crime from that age group persisted in between 70000 and 80000. Soon as 2021 ended it shows that the crimes of that age group soared well over 80000. Another comparison that could be made is the 45-64 age group. Based on the 2020 histogram of their age group they were a little under 30000, judging on the 2021 histogram though there can be seen a little bit of increase that might of pushed past the 30000 threshold.

A Map Visualization of 1000 Crime

Though only 1000 rows were used to create this map visualization it shows how compact the amount of crimes are surrounding the city. To a point that looking at Staten Island you could see how little crime there is there compared to the other boroughs.

Conclusive Prediction

As sad as it is to admit, it seems crimes happening around NYC are not going to be decreasing at any time soon. Based on the research of 2020 and 2021, there seems to be a steady climbing of crime uprising. Though crime is rising,  you , could make a difference. Whether it's one crime you report, or two. If everyone speaks up around and help spread awareness of crime it'll surely decrease. Don't forget, crime is everywhere around us and always stay focused on your surroundings.

Techniques Implemented

Most of the techniques I used were surrounding the module matplotlib.pyplot to create the three plots. Not much editing was required within the data set since a lot of the information helped auto fill the models. The hardest part of the project was to use the Historic dataset and only filter out all other years but 2020. In order to do so, I had to create a new column called Arrest Year which derived from Arrest Date. Drop all NaN values and change the type of the column to int before using panda's loc function to filter it all out. Another challenging aspect of the project came from sorting the boroughs by how much crimes they've each commited. I created a function called sort_boro which was ran after using panda's replace function. By using the replace function it changed all values in the column Arrest Boro to their respective borough. After doing so, the function would count and return an array of 5 digits which held the amount of crime each borough commited.
